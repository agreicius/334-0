Theorem 1.21.8. Adjoint of a matrix transformation.

Assume \(F\in \{\R,\C\}\text{.}\) Let \(T\in\mathcal{L}(F^n, F^m)\text{,}\) and let \(A\in F^{m,n}\) be the unique matrix such that \(T=T_A\text{.}\) The adjoint \(T^*\in \mathcal{L}(F^m, F^n)\text{,}\) taken with respect to the standard inner products on \(F^n\) and \(F^m\text{,}\) is the matrix transformation \(T_{A^*}\text{.}\)

Proof.

By uniqueness of the adjoint, it suffices to show that \(T_{A^*}\) satisfies the adjoint property: that is, that
\begin{align*} \angvec{T_A(\boldx),\boldy} \amp =\angvec{\boldx,T_{A^*}\boldy} \end{align*}
for all \(\boldx\in F^n\) and \(\boldy\in F^m\text{.}\) We have
\begin{align*} \angvec{\boldx,T_{A^*}\boldy} \amp =\angvec{\boldx,A^*\boldy} \\ \amp = (A^*\boldy)^*\boldx \amp (\knowl{./knowl/xref/th_adjoint_inner.html}{\text{1.21.7}})\\ \amp =\boldy^* (A^*)^*\boldx \amp \knowl{./knowl/xref/eq_adjoint_prod.html}{\text{(1.90)}} \\ \amp = \boldy^* A\boldx \amp \knowl{./knowl/xref/eq_adj_adj_matrix.html}{\text{(1.91)}}\\ \amp = \angvec{A\boldx, \boldy} \amp (\knowl{./knowl/xref/th_adjoint_inner.html}{\text{1.21.7}})\\ \amp = \angvec{T_A(\boldx), \boldy}\text{.} \end{align*}
