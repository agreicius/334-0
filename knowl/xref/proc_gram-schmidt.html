Procedure 1.19.5. Gram-Schmidt procedure.

Let \((V,\angvec{\, ,})\) be an inner product space of dimension \(n\text{,}\) and let \(B=(v_1, v_2, \dots, v_n)\) be a basis for \(V\text{.}\) To produce an orthogonal basis \(B'\) (or orthonormal basis \(B''\)) from \(B\text{,}\) follow the steps below.
  1. Set \(w_1=v_1\text{.}\)
  2. Orthogonalize.
    Proceeding in succession for each \(2\leq r\leq n\text{,}\) replace \(v_r\) with the vector \(w_r\) defined as
    \begin{equation*} w_r=v_r-\frac{\angvec{v_r, w_{r-1}}}{\angvec{w_{r-1},w_{r-1}}}w_{r-1}-\frac{\angvec{v_r, w_{r-2}}}{\angvec{w_{r-2},w_{r-2}}}w_{r-2}-\cdots -\frac{\angvec{v_r, w_{1}}}{\angvec{w_{1},w_{1}}}w_1\text{.} \end{equation*}
    The resulting tuple \(B'=(w_1, w_2, \dots, w_n)\) is an orthogonal basis of \(V\text{.}\)
  3. Normalize.
    For each \(1\leq i\leq n\) let
    \begin{equation*} u_i=\frac{1}{\norm{w_i}}\,w_i\text{.} \end{equation*}
    The tuple \(B''=(u_1, u_2, \dots, u_n)\) is an orthonormal basis of \(V\text{.}\)
