Theorem 1.22.7. Normality preserved.

Let \((V,\angvec{\, ,})\) be an inner product space. Let \(\operatorname{Norm}(V)\text{,}\) \(\operatorname{SelfAdj}(V)\text{,}\) \(\operatorname{Skew}(V)\text{,}\) and \(\operatorname{Uni}(V)\) denote the sets of normal, self-adjoint, skew-Hermitian, and unitary operators on \(V\text{,}\) respectively.
  1. \(T\in \operatorname{Norm}(V)/\operatorname{SelfAdj}(V)/\operatorname{Skew}(V)/\operatorname{Uni}(V)\) if and only if \(T^*\in\operatorname{Norm}(V)/\operatorname{SelfAdj}(V)/\operatorname{Skew}(V)/\operatorname{Uni}(V)\text{.}\)
  2. If \(S,T\in \operatorname{SelfAdj}(V) \text{,}\) then \(cS+dT\in \operatorname{SelfAdj}(V)\) for all \(c,d\in \R\text{.}\)
  3. If \(T\in \operatorname{Norm}(V)/\operatorname{SelfAdj}(V)/\operatorname{Uni}(V)\text{,}\) then \(T^k\in \operatorname{Norm}(V)/\operatorname{SelfAdj}(V)/\operatorname{Uni}(V)\) for any positive integer \(k\text{.}\)

Proof.
