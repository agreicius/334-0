Theorem 1.22.7. Normality preserved.

Let \((V,\angvec{\, ,})\) be an inner product space. Let \(\operatorname{Norm}(V)\text{,}\) \(\operatorname{Sym}(V)\text{,}\) \(\operatorname{Skew}(V)\text{,}\) and \(\operatorname{Uni}(V)\) denote the sets of normal, self-adjoint, skew-Hermitian, and unitary operators on \(V\text{,}\) respectively, and let \(\mathcal{P}\) denote any one of these sets of operators.
  1. \(T\in \mathcal{P}\) if and only if \(T^*\in \mathcal{P}\text{.}\)
  2. If \(S,T\in\mathcal{P}\text{,}\) then \(cS+dT\in \mathcal{P}\) for all \(c,d\in F\text{.}\)
  3. If \(T\in \mathcal{P}\text{,}\) then \(T^k\in \mathcal{P}\) for any positive integer \(k\text{.}\)

Proof.
