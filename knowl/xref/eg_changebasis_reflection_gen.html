Example 1.14.16. Reflection in \(\R^2\text{:}\) general.

Let \(\ell=\Span((a,b))\) be the line in \(\R^2\) passing through the origin with nonzero direction vector \((a,b)\text{,}\) and let \(T\colon \R^2\rightarrow \R^2\) be reflection through \(\ell\text{:}\) i.e., given point \(P=(x,y)\text{,}\) \(T(P)\) is the point \(P'=(x',y')\) obtained by reflecting \(P\) through \(\ell\text{.}\)
  1. Generalize the technique in ExampleÂ 1.14.15 to derive the standard matrix \(A\) of \(T\text{.}\)
  2. Give the formula for \(T(x,y)\) and verify that it computes the reflection of \((a,b)\) and \((b,-a)\) correctly.
Solution.
The standard matrix of \(T\) is just \(A=[T]_B\text{,}\) where \(B\) is the standard basis of \(\R^2\text{.}\) Let \(B'=(\boldv_1=(a,b),\boldv_2=(b,-a))\text{.}\) Since \((a,b)\in \ell\text{,}\) we have \(T(a,b)=(a,b)\text{;}\) since \((b,-a)\) is perpendicular to \(\ell\text{,}\) we have \(T(b,-a)=-(b,-a)\text{.}\) It follows that
\begin{align*} [T]_{B'} \amp = \begin{bmatrix} \vert \amp \vert\\ [T(\boldv_1)]_{B'}\amp [T(\boldv_2)]_{B'}\\ \vert \amp \vert \end{bmatrix}= \begin{bmatrix} \vert \amp \vert\\ [\boldv_1]_{B'}\amp [-\boldv_2]_{B'}\\ \vert \amp \vert \end{bmatrix}= \begin{bmatrix} 1\amp 0 \\ 0\amp -1 \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{align*}
Computing the relevant change of basis matrices and using the change of basis formula (1.41), we have
\begin{align*} A=[T]_B \amp = \underset{B'\to B}{P}\, [T]_{B'}\,\underset{B\to B'}{P}\\ \amp = \frac{1}{a^2+b^2}\begin{amatrix}[rr] a\amp b\\ b\amp -a \end{amatrix}\begin{bmatrix} 1\amp 0\\ 0\amp -1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{amatrix} a\amp b \\ b\amp -a \end{amatrix} \\ \amp =\frac{1}{a^2+b^2}\begin{amatrix} a^2-b^2\amp 2ab\\ 2ab \amp b^2-a^2 \end{amatrix}\text{.} \end{align*}
Since \(T=T_A\text{,}\) we derive the formula
\begin{equation*} T(x,y)=\frac{1}{a^2+b^2}((a^2-b^2)x+2aby, 2abx+(b^2-a^2)y) \end{equation*}
for all \((x,y)\in\R^2\text{.}\) In particular, we have
\begin{align*} T(a,b) \amp =\frac{1}{a^2+b^2}((a^2-b^2)a+2ab^2, 2a^2b+(b^2-a^2)b)\\ \amp =(a,b)\\ T(b,-a) \amp =\frac{1}{a^2+b^2}((a^2-b^2)b-2a^2b, 2ab^2-(b^2-a^2)a)\\ \amp =-(b,-a)\text{,} \end{align*}
as expected.
