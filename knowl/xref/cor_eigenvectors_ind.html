Corollary 1.17.12. Independence of eigenvectors.

Let \(T\) be a linear operator on the vector space \(V\) (not necessarily finite dimensional). If \(\lambda_1,\lambda_2,\dots, \lambda_r\) are distinct eigenvalues of \(T\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation} \sum_{i=1}^rE(\lambda_i, T)=\bigoplus_{i=1}^r E(\lambda_i, T)\text{.}\tag{1.56} \end{equation}

Proof.

Suppose we have a decomposition of $\boldzero$ of the form
\begin{equation} \boldzero=v_1+v_2+\cdots +v_r, v_i\in E(\lambda_i, T)\text{.}\tag{1.57} \end{equation}
Let $J=\{j\in \{1,2,\dots, r\}\colon v_j\ne \boldzero\}$. If $J$ is nonempty, then $v_j$ is a $\lambda_j$-eigenvector, and we have
\begin{equation*} \boldzero=\sum_{j\in J}v_j\text{,} \end{equation*}
a nontrivial linear combination of the tuple \(S=(v_j)_{j\in J}\text{.}\) This is a contradiction, as this tuple of eigenvectors (with distinct eigenvalues) is linearly independent by Theorem 1.17.10. Thus \(J\) is empty, and we see that the decomposition (1.57) must be the trivial one. We conclude from Theorem 1.8.13 that subspace sum \(W=\sum_{i=1}^rE(\lambda_i, T)\) is direct: i.e., that
\begin{equation*} \sum_{i=1}^rE(\lambda_i, T)=\bigoplus_{i=1}^rE(\lambda_i, T)\text{,} \end{equation*}
as desired.
