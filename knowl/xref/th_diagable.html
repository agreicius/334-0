Theorem 1.17.4. Diagonalizable operators.

Let \(T\) be a linear operator on the finite-dimensional vector space \(V\text{,}\) and let \(\lambda_1,\lambda_2,\dots, \lambda_r\) be the distinct eigenvalues of \(T\text{.}\) The following statements are equivalent.
  1. \(T\) is diagonalizable.
  2. \(T\) has an eigenbasis \(B\text{.}\)
  3. \(V=\bigoplus_{i=1}^r E(\lambda_i, T)\text{.}\)
  4. \(\dim V=\sum_{i=1}^r\dim E(\lambda_i, T)\text{.}\)
