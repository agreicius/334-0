Example 1.23.9. Normal operator (\(F=\C\)).

Let \(A=\begin{bmatrix} 0\amp 1\\ -1 \amp 0 \end{bmatrix}\) and consider the matrix transformation \(T_A\colon \C^2\rightarrow \C^2\text{,}\) where \(\C^2\) is given the standard Hermitian inner product.
  1. Show that \(T_A\) is normal, skew-Hermitian, and unitary!
  2. Verify that (2) of Theorem 1.23.8 applies for \(T_A\text{.}\)
Solution.
  1. We have
    \begin{equation*} T_A^*=T_{A^*}\text{,} \end{equation*}
    where
    \begin{equation*} A^*=\overline{A^T}=\begin{bmatrix} 0\amp -1 \\ 1 \amp 0 \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}
    It is easily verified that
    \begin{equation*} A^*=-A=A^{-1}\text{,} \end{equation*}
    and hence that \(A\) is skew-Hermitian and unitary, and hence also normal.
  2. The usual computations show that the characteristic polynomial of \(T_A\) is
    \begin{equation*} f(x)=x^2+1=(x-i)(x+i)\text{,} \end{equation*}
    and that the nonzero eigenspaces of \(T_A\) are
    \begin{align*} E(i,T_A) \amp =\Span(1,i)\\ E(-i,T_A) \amp = \Span(1,-i)\text{.} \end{align*}
    Since \(\dim \C^2=2=\dim E(i,T_A)+\dim E(-i,T_A)\text{,}\) we see that \(V=E(i,T_A)\oplus E(-i,T_A)\) by Theorem 1.17.4. Lastly since
    \begin{equation*} \angvec{(1,i),(1,-i)}=1\cdot 1+i\cdot\overline{-i}=1+i^2=1-1=0\text{.} \end{equation*}
    it follows that \(E(i,T_A)\) and \(E(-i,T_A)\) are orthogonal.
