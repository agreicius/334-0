Example 1.16.8. Rotation (again).

Fix an angle \(\theta\in [0,2\pi)\text{,}\) and let
\begin{equation*} A=\begin{amatrix}[rr] \cos\theta\amp -\sin \theta \\ \sin\theta \amp \cos\theta \end{amatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}
As mentioned earlier, the linear operator \(T_A\in\mathcal{L}(\R^2)\) is rotation about the origin by the angle \(\theta\text{.}\)
  1. Compute the characteristic polynomial of \(A\text{.}\)
  2. Find all eigenvalues of \(T_A\in \mathcal{L}(\R^2)\text{.}\) Consider the cases \(\theta=0\text{,}\) \(\theta=\pi\text{,}\) and \(\theta\ne 0,\pi\) separately.
Solution.
The characteristic polynomial of \(A\) is
\begin{align*} f(x)=\det(xI-A) \amp= \det \begin{bmatrix} x-\cos\theta\amp \sin\theta\\ -\sin\theta\amp x-\cos\theta \end{bmatrix} \\ \amp=x^2-2(\cos\theta)x+1\text{.} \end{align*}
We can use the quadratic formula to find the real roots of \(f\text{:}\)
\begin{align*} x \amp = \frac{2\cos\theta\pm \sqrt{4\cos^2\theta-4}}{2}\\ \amp = \cos\theta\pm \sqrt{\cos^2\theta-1}\\ \amp = \cos\theta\pm \sqrt{-\sin^2\theta}\text{.} \end{align*}
When \(\theta=0\text{,}\) this reduces to \(x=\cos 0=1\text{;}\) similarly, when \(\theta=\pi\text{,}\) this reduces to \(x=\cos\pi=-1\text{.}\) This confirms our our conclusion in Example 1.15.8 that when \(\theta=\pi\) the only eigenvalue of \(A\) is \(0\text{;}\) and that when \(\theta=\pi\text{,}\) the only eigenvalue of \(A\) is \(-1\text{.}\)
When \(\theta\in [0\2pi)-\{0,\pi\}\text{,}\) \(-\sin^2\theta< 0\) and we see that \(f(x)\) has no real roots. This confirms our conclusion in Example 1.15.8 that such rotations have no eigenvalues.
