Corollary 1.21.11. Matrix representation of adjoint.

Let \((V,\angvec{\, ,}_V)\) be an inner product space of finite dimension \(n\text{,}\) and let \(B\) be an orthonormal basis of \(V\text{.}\) Given \(T\in \mathcal{L}(V)\) and adjoint \(T^*\in\mathcal{L}(V)\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation} [T^*]_{B}=\left([T]_B\right)^*\text{.}\tag{1.99} \end{equation}
More generally, given \(S\in \mathcal{L}(V)\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation} S=T^* \iff [S]_B=\left([T]_B\right)^*\text{.}\tag{1.100} \end{equation}

Proof.

It suffices to show the equivalence (1.100). The key ingredients to the proof are summarized as follows:
  1. Since \(B\) is an orthormal basis, we have
    \begin{align*} \angvec{v,v'}_V \amp =\angvec{[v]_B,[v']_B]}\text{,} \end{align*}
    where the inner products on the right is the standard one on \(F^{n}\text{.}\)
  2. Matrices \(A,C\in F^{m,n}\) satisfy
    \begin{equation*} \angvec{A\boldx,\boldy}=\angvec{\boldx, C\boldy} \end{equation*}
    for all \(\boldx\in F^{n}\) and \(\boldy\in F^{m}\) if and only if \(C=A^*\text{.}\) This follows from TheoremÂ 1.21.9 and uniqueness of the adjoint (if it exists).
We now prove the equivalence:
\begin{align*} S=T^* \amp \iff \angvec{T(v),w}=\angvec{v,S(w)} \text{ for all } v,w\in V \hspace{10pt} (\text{def. of adj.})\\ \amp \iff \angvec{[T(v)]_B,[w]_{B}}=\angvec{[v]_B,[S(w)]_B} \text{ for all } v,w\in V \hspace{10pt} (\text{by (i) above}) \\ \amp \iff \angvec{[T]_B[v]_{B},[w]_{B}}=\angvec{[v]_B,[S]_{B}[w]_B} \text{ for all } v,w\in V \hspace{10pt} \knowl{./knowl/xref/eq_matrixrep_prop.html}{\text{(1.35)}}\\ \amp \iff \angvec{[T]_B\,\boldx,\boldy}=\angvec{\boldx,[S]_{B}\,\boldy} \text{ for all } \boldx,\boldy\in F^n\hspace{10pt} ([\phantom{v}]_B \text{ an isom.}) \\ \amp \iff [S]_B=\left([T]_B\right)^* \hspace{10pt} (\text{by (ii) above}) \text{.} \end{align*}
