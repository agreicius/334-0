Theorem 1.26.5. Jordan form.

Let \(T\in \mathcal{L}(V)\) where \(V\) is finite dimensional, and suppose all roots of the characteristic polynomial of \(T\) lie in \(\F\text{.}\)
  1. There is a Jordan basis \(B\) for \(T\text{.}\)
  2. The corresponding Jordan form
    \begin{equation*} A=[T]_B=\begin{bmatrix} J_1\amp \\ \amp J_2\amp \\ \amp \amp\ddots \\ \amp \amp \amp J_r \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
    is unique up to a permutation of the Jordan blocks \(J_{i}\text{.}\)

Proof.
