Example 1.9.18. Function evaluation.

Let \(X\) be a nonempty set, and let \((x_1,x_2,\dots, x_n)\) a list of \(n\) elements of \(X\text{.}\) Prove that the function
\begin{align*} T\colon F^X \amp \rightarrow F^n\\ f \amp \mapsto (f(x_1),f(x_2),\dots, f(x_n)) \end{align*}
is a linear transformation.
Solution.
For all \(f,g\in F^X\) and scalars \(c,d\in F\text{,}\) we have
\begin{align*} T(cf+dg) \amp =((cf+dg)(x_1),(cf+dg)(x_2),\dots, (cf+dg)(x_n))\\ \amp = (cf(x_1)+dg(x_1),cf(x_2)+dg(x_2),\dots, cf(x_n)+dg(x_n))\\ \amp =c(f(x_1),f(x_2),\dots, f(x_n))+d(g(x_1),g(x_2),\dots, g(x_n))\\ \amp =cT(f)+dT(g)\text{.} \end{align*}
