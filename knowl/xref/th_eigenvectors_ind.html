Theorem 1.17.10. Independence of eigenvectors.

Let \(T\) be a linear operator on the vector space \(V\) (not necessarily finite dimensional). If \(S=(v_i)_{i\in I}\) is a tuple of eigenvectors of \(T\) satisfying \(T(v_i)=\lambda_i v_i\) for all \(i\in I\) and \(\lambda_i\ne \lambda_j\) for all \(i\ne j\) (i.e., the eigenvalues are distinct), then \(S\) is linearly independent.

Proof.

We prove the result by contradiction. If \(S=(v_i)_{i\in I}\) is linearly depenendent, then we can find a linearly dependent finite subtuple \(S'=(v_{i_1}, v_{i_2},\dots, v_{i_r})\) of minimal length \(r\text{.}\) Since each \(v_{i_j}\) is an eigenvector of \(T\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} T(v_{i_j})=\lambda_j v_{i_j} \end{equation*}
for all \(1\leq j\leq r\text{;}\) since the eigenvalues are assumed to be distinct, we have \(\lambda_j\ne \lambda_\ell\) for \(j\ne \ell\text{.}\)
Note first that we must have \(r\geq 2\text{:}\) since eigenvectors are nonzero by definition, no subtuple of \(S\) of length 1 is linearly dependent. Since \(S'\) is linearly dependent we have
\begin{equation} c_1v_{i_1}+c_2v_{i_2}+\cdots +c_rv_{i_r}=\boldzero\text{,}\tag{1.50} \end{equation}
where \(c_i\ne 0\) for some \(1\leq i\leq r\text{.}\) After reordering, we may assume without loss of generality that \(c_1\ne 0\text{.}\) Next we apply \(T\) to both sides of (1.50):
\begin{align} c_1v_{i_1}+c_2v_{i_2}\cdots +c_rv_{i_r}\amp=\boldzero \tag{1.51}\\ T(c_1v_{i_1}+c_2v_{i_2}+\cdots +c_rv_{i_r})\amp=T(\boldzero)\tag{1.52}\\ c_1T(v_{i_1})+c_2T(v_{i_2})+\cdots +c_rT(v_{i_r})\amp =\boldzero \tag{1.53}\\ c_1\lambda_1v_{i_1}+c_2\lambda_2v_{i_2}+\cdots +c_r\lambda_rv_{i_r}=\boldzero\text{.}\tag{1.54} \end{align}
From equation (1.50) and the equation in (1.54) we have
\begin{equation*} \lambda_r(c_1v_{i_1}+c_2v_{i_2}\cdots +c_rv_{i_r})- (c_1\lambda_1v_{i_1}+c_2\lambda_2v_{i_2}\cdots +c_r\lambda_rv_{i_r})=\boldzero\text{,} \end{equation*}
and hence
\begin{equation} c_1(\lambda_r-\lambda_1)v_{i_1}+\cdots +c_{r-1}(\lambda_r-\lambda_{r-1})v_{i_{r-1}}+\cancel{c_r(\lambda_r-\lambda_r)}v_{i_r}=\boldzero\text{.}\tag{1.55} \end{equation}
Since \(c_1\ne 0\) and \(\lambda_1\ne \lambda_r\text{,}\) we have \(c_1(\lambda_r-\lambda_1)\ne 0\text{.}\) Thus equation (1.55) implies that the tuple \(S''=(v_{i_1}, v_{i_2}, \dots, v_{i_{r-1}})\) is a linearly dependent subtuple of \(S\) of length \(r=1\text{,}\) contradicting the minimality of \(r\text{.}\) Since we have reached a contradiction, we conclude that \(S\) must be linearly independent.
