Corollary 1.22.10. Normal operator properties.

Let \((V,\angvec{\, ,})\) be an inner product space, and assume \(T\in \mathcal{L}(V)\) is normal.
  1. \(T(v)=\boldzero\) if and only if \(T^*(v)=\boldzero\text{.}\) As a consequence, we have
    \begin{equation} \NS T=\NS T^*\text{.}\tag{1.108} \end{equation}
  2. \(T(v)=\lambda v\) if and only if \(T^*(v)=\overline{\lambda}v\text{.}\) As a consequence, we have
    \begin{equation} E(\lambda, T)=E(\overline{\lambda},T^*)\tag{1.109} \end{equation}
    for all \(\lambda\in F\text{.}\)
  3. Finite-dimensional space.
    Assume further that \(\dim V=n< \infty\text{.}\) We have
    \begin{equation} \im T=\im T^*\tag{1.110} \end{equation}
    and
    \begin{equation} V=\ker T\oplus \im T=\ker T^*\oplus \im T^*\text{.}\tag{1.111} \end{equation}

Proof.

Assume \(T\) is normal.
  1. We have
    \begin{align*} v\in \NS T \amp \iff T(v)=\boldzero\\ \amp \iff \norm{T(v)}=0\\ \amp \iff \norm{T^*(v)}=0\\ \amp \iff T^*(v)=\boldzero\\ \amp \iff v\in \NS T^*\text{.} \end{align*}
    Thus \(\NS T=\NS T^*\text{.}\)
  2. Since \(T\) and \(I_V\) are normal, so is, \(\lambda I_V-T\) for any \(\lambda\in F\) by TheoremÂ 1.22.7. Applying the result in (1) to \(\lambda I_V-T\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{align*} T(v)=\lambda v \amp \iff v\in E(\lambda, T) \\ \amp \iff v\in \NS (\lambda I_V-T)\\ \amp \iff v\in \NS(\lambda I_V-T)^*\\ \amp \iff v\in \NS(\overline{\lambda}I_V-T^*) \amp \knowl{./knowl/xref/th_adjoint_props.html}{\text{Theorem 1.21.5}} \\ \amp \iff v\in E(\overline{\lambda},T^*) \\ \amp \iff T^*(v)=\overline{\lambda}v\text{.} \end{align*}
  3. If we assume \(V\) is finite dimensional, then we have
    \begin{align*} \im T^* \amp =(\NS T)^\perp\\ \amp =(\NS T^*)^\perp\\ \amp = ((\im T)^\perp)^\perp\\ \amp =\im T\text{.} \end{align*}
    It follows that
    \begin{align*} V \amp = \NS T\oplus (\NS T)^\perp\\ \amp = \NS T \oplus \im T^*\\ \amp =\NS T\oplus \im T\\ \amp =\NS T^*\oplus \im T^*\text{.} \end{align*}
