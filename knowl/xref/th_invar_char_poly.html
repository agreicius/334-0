Theorem 1.24.2. Invariant subspaces and characteristic polynomial.

Let \(T\in \mathcal{L}(V)\text{,}\) where \(V\) is finite dimensional, and suppose \(W\) be a \(T\)-invariant subspace of \(V\text{.}\) Let \(f\) be the characteristic polynomial of \(T\text{,}\) and let \(g\) be the characteristic polynomial of the restriction \(T\vert_W\text{.}\) We have \(g\mid f\text{:}\) i.e., the characteristic polynomial of \(T\vert_W\) divides the characteristic polynomial of \(T\) for any \(T\)-invariant subspace \(W\text{.}\)

Proof.

Let \(B'=(v_1,v_2,\dots, v_k)\) be a basis of \(W\text{,}\) and extend to a basis \(B=(v_1,v_2,\dots, v_n)\text{.}\) Since \(W\) is \(T\)-invariant, it follows easily from (1.34) that
\begin{equation*} [T]_B=\begin{bmatrix} \underset{k\times k}{C} \amp \underset{k\times (n-k)}{D}\\ \underset{(n-k)\times k}{\boldzero} \amp \underset{(n-k)\times (n-k)}{E} \end{bmatrix}\text{,} \end{equation*}
and that furthermore, \(C=[T\vert_W]_{B'}\text{.}\) A matrix of the form \(A=[T]_B\) above is called block upper diagonal. An easy induction argument shows that in general the determinant of block upper diagonal matrices is the product of the determinants of the block matrices along the diagonal. As a result we have
\begin{align*} \det(xI-A)\amp =\det \begin{bmatrix} \underset{k\times k}{xI-C} \amp \underset{k\times (n-k)}{-D}\\ \underset{(n-k)\times k}{\boldzero} \amp \underset{(n-k)\times (n-k)}{xI-E} \end{bmatrix} \amp \\ \amp =\det(xI-C)\det(cI-E)\text{.} \end{align*}
Since the characteristic polynomial of \(T\) is \(f(x)=\det(xI-A)\text{,}\) and the characteristic polynomial of \(T\vert_W\) is \(g(x)=\det(xI-C)\) (\(C=[T\vert_W]_{B'}\)), we conclude that \(g\mid f\text{.}\)
