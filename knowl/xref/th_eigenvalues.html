Theorem 1.15.9. Eigenvalues and null spaces.

Let \(T\in \mathcal{L}(V)\text{,}\) and let \(\lambda \in F\text{.}\) The following statements are equivalent.
  1. \(\lambda\) is an eigenvalue of \(T\text{.}\)
  2. \(T-\lambda I_V\) is not injective.
  3. \(\NS (T-\lambda I_V)\ne \{\boldzero\}\text{.}\)

Proof.

We have
\begin{align*} \lambda \text{ eigenvalue of } T \amp \iff T(v)=\lambda v \text{ for some nonzero} v\in V\\ \amp\iff T(v)=\lambdaI_V(v) \text{ for some nonzero} v\in V \\ \amp \iff (T-\lambda I_V)(v)=\boldzero \text{ for some nonzero} v\in V\\ \amp \iff \NS(T-\lambda I_V)\ne \{\boldzero\}\\ \amp \iff T-\lambda I_V \text{ not injective}\text{.} \end{align*}
