Corollary 1.17.13. Distinct eigenvalues.

Let \(T\in \mathcal{L}(V)\text{,}\) where \(\dim V=n< \infty\text{.}\) If \(T\) has \(n\) distinct eigenvalues, then \(T\) is diagonalizable.

Proof.

Let \(\lambda_1,\lambda_2,\dots, \lambda_n\) be the \(n\) distict eigenvalues of \(T\text{,}\) and let \(B=(v_1,v_2,\dots, v_n)\) be a tuple of eigenvectors satisfying \(T(v_i)=\lambda_i\) for all \(1\leq i\leq n\text{.}\) By Theorem 1.17.10, \(B\) is linearly independent. Since \(\dim V=n=\len B\text{,}\) it follows that \(B\) is an eigenbasis of \(V\text{.}\) From Theorem 1.17.4, we conclude that \(T\) is diagonalizable.
