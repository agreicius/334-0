Corollary 1.24.12. Minimal polynomial and characteristic polynomial.

Let \(T\in \mathcal{L}(V)\text{,}\) where \(V\) is finite dimensional, let \(f\) and \(m_T\) be the characteristic and minimal polynomials of \(T\text{,}\) respectively. If \(f\) splits completely over \(F\) as
\begin{equation*} f(x)=\prod_{i=1}^r(x-\lambda_i)^{n_i}, n_i\geq 1\text{,} \end{equation*}
then \(m_T(x)\) splits completely as
\begin{equation*} m_T(x)=\prod_{i=1}^{r}(x-\lambda_i)^{m_i}\text{,} \end{equation*}
where \(1\leq m_i\leq n_i\) for all \(1\leq i\leq r\text{.}\) In other words, \(m_T\) and \(f\) both split completely over \(F\) and have the same set of roots.
