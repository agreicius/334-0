Corollary 1.24.11. Minimal polynomial and characteristic polynomial.

Let \(T\in \mathcal{L}(V)\text{,}\) where \(V\) is finite dimensional, let \(f\) and \(m_T\) be the characteristic and minimal polynomials of \(T\text{,}\) respectively. If \(f\) splits completely over \(F\) as
\begin{equation*} f(x)=\prod_{i=1}^r(x-\lambda_i)^{n_i}, n_i\geq 1\text{,} \end{equation*}
then \(m_T(x)\) splits completely as
\begin{equation*} m_T(x)=\prod_{i=1}^{r}(x-\lambda_i)^{m_i}\text{,} \end{equation*}
where \(1\leq m_i\leq n_i\) for all \(1\leq i\leq r\text{.}\) In other words, \(m_T\) and \(f\) both split completely over \(F\) and have the same set of roots.

Proof.

Since \(m_T\mid f\text{,}\) unique factorization of polynomials implies
\begin{equation*} m_T(x)=\prod_{j\in J}(x-\lambda_{j})^{m_{j}} \end{equation*}
for some subset \(J\subseteq I=\{1,2,\dots, r\}\text{,}\) and exponents \(m_{j}\leq n_{j}\text{.}\) We will show that \(J=I\text{:}\) equivalently, we will show that \((x-\lambda_i)\mid m_T(x)\) for all \(1\leq i\leq r\text{.}\) Fix \(1\leq i\leq r\) and eigenvector \(v\in E(\lambda_i, T)\text{.}\) Since \(T(v)=\lambda_i v\text{,}\) we have \((T-\lambda I)v=(\lambda_i-\lambda)v\text{,}\) and hence \((T-\lambda I)^k(v)=(\lambda_i-\lambda)^kv\text{.}\) It follows that
\begin{align*} m_T(T)(v) \amp =\prod_{j\in J}(T-\lambda_j)^{m_{j}}(v)\\ \amp = \left(\prod_{j\in J}(\lambda_i-\lambda_{j})^{m_{j}}\right)v \end{align*}
Since \(m_T(T)(v)=\boldzero\text{,}\) and since \(v\ne \boldzero\text{,}\) we must have
\begin{equation*} \prod_{j\in J}(\lambda_i-\lambda_{j})^{m_{j}}=0\text{.} \end{equation*}
But this is true if and only if \(\lambda_i=\lambda_{j}\) for some \(j\in J\text{,}\) or equivalently, \(i\in J\text{.}\)
