Theorem 1.21.9. Adjoint properties.

Let \((V,\angvec{\, }_V)\text{,}\) \((W,\angvec{\, }_W)\text{,}\) \((U,\angvec{\, ,}_U\) be inner product spaces.
  1. Assume \(V\) and \(W\) are finite-dimensional. If \(T\in \mathcal{L}(V,W)\) and \(S\in \mathcal{L}(W,U)\text{,}\) then
    \begin{equation} (ST)^*=T^*S^*\text{.}\tag{1.87} \end{equation}
  2. Assume \(V\) and \(W\) are finite dimensional. If \(T\in \mathcal{L}(V,W)\text{,}\) then
    \begin{equation} (T^*)^*=T\text{.}\tag{1.88} \end{equation}
  3. Assume \(V\) is finite dimensional. Given \(T\in \mathcal{L}(V,W)\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{align*} \NS T^* \amp =\im T^\perp\\ \im T^* \amp =\NS T^\perp \end{align*}

Proof.
