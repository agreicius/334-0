Theorem 1.8.7. Basis bounds.

Let \(B\) be a basis of the vector space \(V\text{,}\) and suppose \(\len B=n< \infty\text{.}\) Let \(S\) be a tuple of vectors of \(V\text{.}\)
  1. If \(S\) spans \(V\text{,}\) then \(\len S\geq n\text{.}\)
  2. If \(S\) is linearly independent, then \(\len S\leq n\text{.}\)
  3. If \(S\) is a basis for \(V\text{,}\) then \(\len S=n\text{.}\)

Proof.

  1. Suppose by contradiction that \(\Span S=V\) and \(\len S < n\text{.}\) It follows from Theorem 1.6.13 that \(B\) is linearly dependent. Since this is a contradiction, we conclude that \(\len S\geq n\text{.}\)
  2. This also follows from Theorem 1.6.13: since \(B\) is a spanning set of \(V\) any of length greater than \(n=\len B\) must be linearly dependent.
  3. This follows from (1) and (2): if \(S\) is a basis, then since \(S\) spans, we have \(\len S\geq n\text{;}\) and since \(B\) is linearly independent we have \(\len S\leq n\text{.}\) We conclude that \(\len S=n\text{.}\)
