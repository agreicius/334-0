Definition 1.7.13. Finite/infinite dimension.

A vector space \(V\) is finite dimensional if there is a finite basis of \(V\text{;}\) the vector space is infinite dimensional if there is no finite basis of \(V\text{.}\) We write \(\dim V< \infty\) to indicate that \(V\) is finite dimensional, and \(\dim V=\infty\) to indicate \(V\) is infinite dimensional.
