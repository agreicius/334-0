Procedure 1.17.5. Diagonalizable operator.

Let \(T\) be a linear operator of the finite-dimensional vector space \(V\text{.}\) To determine whether \(T\) is diagonalizable and/or find an eigenbasis of \(T\text{,}\) proceed as follows.
  1. Determine the distinct eigenvalues \(\lambda_1,\lambda_2,\dots, \lambda_r\) of \(T\text{.}\)
  2. For each \(1\leq i\leq r\text{,}\) compute a basis \(B_i\) for \(E(\lambda_i, T)\text{.}\)
  3. \(T\) is diagonalizable if and only if \(\sum_{i=1}^r\dim E(\lambda_i, T)=\dim V\text{.}\)
  4. If \(T\) is diagonalizable, then the tuple \(B=B_1*B_2*\cdots *B_r\) obtained by concatenating the bases \(B_i\) is an eigenbasis of \(T\text{.}\)
