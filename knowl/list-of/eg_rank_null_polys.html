Example 1.10.13. Polynomial evaluation.

Let \(n\) be a positive integer, and let \(\boldc=(c_1,c_2,\dots, c_{n+1})\in \C^{n+1}\) be a choice of \(n+1\) complex inputs. Prove that for any choice \(\boldd=(d_1,d_2,\dots, d_{n+1})\in \C^{n+1}\) of \(n+1\) complex outputs there is a unique polynomial \(f\in P_n(\C)\) satisfying
\begin{align*} f(c_1) \amp = d_1\\ f(c_2) \amp = d_2\\ \amp \vdots \\ f(c_n) \amp = d_n\text{.} \end{align*}
In other words, given any choice of values \(d_k\text{,}\) we can construct a unique polynomia \(f\in P_n(\C)\) whose value at \(c_k\) is \(d_k\) for all \(1\leq k\leq n\text{.}\)
