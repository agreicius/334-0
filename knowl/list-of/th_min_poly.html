Theorem 1.24.11. Minimal polynomial.

Let \(T\in \mathcal{L}(V)\text{,}\) where \(V\) is finite dimensional and let \(m_T(x)\) be the minimal polymonial of \(T\text{.}\) If \(g\in P(F)\) satisfies \(g(T)=0_V\text{,}\) then \(m_T\mid g\text{:}\) i.e., we have
\begin{equation*} g(x)=q(x)m_T(x) \end{equation*}
for some \(q\in P(F)\text{.}\) In particular, if \(f\) is the characteristic polynomial of \(g\text{,}\) then \(m_T(x)\mid f\text{.}\)
