Example 1.9.16. Differentiation.

Let \(I\) be an interval of \(\R\text{.}\) Prove that
\begin{align*} T\colon C^1(I) \amp \rightarrow C(I)\\ f \amp \longmapsto f' \end{align*}
is a linear transformation.
Solution.
The linearity of \(T\) follows from elementary properties of differentiation. We use the one-step technique. For any \(f,g\in C^1(I)\) and scalars \(c,d\in F\text{,}\) we have
\begin{align*} T(cf+dg) \amp = (cf+dg)'\\ \amp =cf'+dg' \amp (\text{props. of diff.})\\ \amp =cT(f)+dT(g)\text{.} \end{align*}
This proves \(T\) is linear.
