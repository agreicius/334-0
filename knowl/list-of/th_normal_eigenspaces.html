Theorem 1.23.7. Eigenspaces of normal operators.

Let \(F\in \{\R, \C\}\text{,}\) and let \(T\) be a normal operator on the finite-dimensional inner product space \((V,\angvec{\, ,})\text{.}\) Given distinct scalars \(\lambda_1\ne \lambda_2\in F\text{,}\) the eigenspaces \(E(\lambda_1,T)\) and \(E(\lambda_2, T)\) are orthogonal: i.e., \(\angvec{v,w}=0\) for all \(v\in E(\lambda_1, T)\) and \(w\in E(\lambda_2,T)\text{.}\)

Proof.

Let \(\lambda_1\) and \(\lambda_2\) be distinct scalars. Take any vectors \(v\in E(\lambda_1, T)\) and \(w\in E(\lambda_2, T)\) and consider first the adjoint property
\begin{equation*} \angvec{T(v),w}=\angvec{v,T^*(w)}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Since \(v\in E(\lambda_1, T)\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} \angvec{T(v),w}=\lambda_1\angvec{v,w}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Since \(T\) is normal, we have \(w\in E(\lambda', T)=E(\overline{\lambda'}, T^*)\) by TheoremÂ 1.18.13, and thus
\begin{equation*} \angvec{v,T^*(w)}=\angvec{v,\overline{\lambda_2}w}=\overline{\overline{\lambda_2}}\angvec{v,w}=\lambda_2\angvec{v,w}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Putting it all together, we have
\begin{equation*} \lambda_1\angvec{v,w}=\angvec{T(v),w}=\angvec{v,T^*(w)}=\lambda_2\angvec{v,w}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Since \(\lambda_1\angvec{v,w}=\lambda_2\angvec{v,w}\) and \(\lambda_1\ne \lambda_2\text{,}\) we conclude that \(\angvec{v,w}=0\text{,}\) as desired.
