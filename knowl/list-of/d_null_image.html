Definition 1.10.1. Null space and image.

Let \(T\colon V\rightarrow W\) be a linear transformation.
  • Null space of \(T\).
    The null space of \(T\) is the subset \(\NS T\) of \(V\) defined as
    \begin{equation} \NS T=\{v\in V\colon T(v)=\boldzero\}\text{.}\tag{1.25} \end{equation}
  • Image of \(T\).
    The image (or range) of \(T\) is the subset \(\im T\) of \(W\) defined as
    \begin{equation} \im T=\{w\in W\colon w=T(v) \text{ for some } v\in V\}\text{.}\tag{1.26} \end{equation}
