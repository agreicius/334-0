Definition 1.25.1. Relatively prime polynomials.

Let \(F\) be a field. Polynomials \(f,g\in P(F)\) are relatively prime if they have no nonconstant common factor: i.e., if \(q\in P(F)\) satisfies \(q\mid f\) and \(q\mid g\text{,}\) then \(q(x)=c\) for some \(c\in F\text{.}\)
More generally a finite list \(f_1,f_2,\dots, f_n\) of polynomials \(f_i\in P(F)\) is relatively prime if there is no nonconstant factor common to all the \(f_i\text{:}\) i.e., if \(q\in P(F)\) satisfies \(q\mid f_i\) for all \(i\text{,}\) then \(q(x)=c\) for some \(c\in F\text{.}\)
