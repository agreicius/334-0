Definition 1.25.4. Generalized eigenspace.

Let \(T\in \mathcal{L}(V)\text{,}\) where \(V\) is finite dimensional. Given \(\lambda\in F\) we define the generalized eigenspace \(G(\lambda, T)\) associated to \(\lambda\) (or \(\lambda\)-generalized eigenspace) to be
\begin{equation} G(\lambda, T)=\{v\in V\colon (T-\lambda I)^k(v)=\boldzero \text{ for some } k\in \Z_+\}\text{.}\tag{1.125} \end{equation}
A generalized \(\lambda\)-eigenvector of \(T\) is a nonzero element of \(G(\lambda, T)\text{.}\)
