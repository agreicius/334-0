Theorem 1.14.2. Change of basis matrix.

Let \(B\) and \(B'\) be two ordered bases of the finite-dimensional vector space \(V\text{.}\)
  1. \(\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}=[I_{V}]_{B}^{B'}\text{.}\)
  2. For all \(v\in V\) we have
    \begin{equation} \underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}[v]_{B}=[v]_{B'}\tag{1.38} \end{equation}
    In other words, to convert the \(B\)-coordinates of a vector \(v\in V\) to \(B'\)-coordinates, simply multiply on the left by the matrix \(\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\text{.}\)
  3. The matrix \(\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\) is the unique matrix satisfying (1.38): i.e., if \(A[v]_{B}=[v]_{B'}\) for all \(v\in V\text{,}\) then \(A=\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\text{.}\)

Proof.

  1. Let \(B=(v_1, v_2,\dots, v_n)\text{.}\) From formula (1.34) applied to \(I_v\text{,}\) we see that the \(j\)-th column of \([I_V]_B^{B'}\) is
    \begin{equation*} [v_j]_{B'} \end{equation*}
    for all \(1\leq j\leq n\text{.}\) Using formula (1.37) from Definition 1.14.1 this is precisely the \(j\)-th column of \(\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\) for all \(1\leq j\leq n\text{.}\) We conclude that
    \begin{equation*} [I_V]_B^{B'} = \underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\text{.} \end{equation*}
  2. This follows from (1) and Theorem 1.13.14:
    \begin{align*} \underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}[v]_B \amp = [\id_V]_{B}^{B'}[v]_B \\ \amp = [\id_V(v)]_{B'} \amp (\knowl{./knowl/xref/th_matrixrep.html}{\text{Theorem 1.13.14}})\\ \amp =[v]_{B'} \text{.} \end{align*}
  3. By (2) of Theorem 1.13.14 (the uniqueness claim), if \(A\) satisfies \(A[v]_B=[v]_{B'}\) for all \(v\in \R^n\text{,}\) then \(A=[\id_V]_{B}^{B'}\text{.}\) Since \([\id_V]_{B}^{B'}=\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\text{,}\) we conclude \(A=\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\text{.}\)
