Corollary 1.8.10. Dimension of subspaces.

Let \(V\) be a vector space.
  1. If \(W\subseteq V\) is a subspace, then \(\dim W\leq \dim V\text{.}\)
  2. If \(W\subseteq V\) is a subspace, then \(\dim W=\dim V\) if and only if \(W=V\text{.}\)

Proof.

  1. Firstly, if \(\dim V=\infty\text{,}\) then clearly \(\dim W\leq \dim V\) for any subspace \(W\subseteq V\text{.}\)
    Now assume \(\dim V=n\text{.}\) Apply the extend to a basis procedure described in the proof of TheoremÂ 1.8.8 to the empty list \(S=()\text{,}\) which is linearly independent, considered as a tuple of vectors in \(W\text{.}\) Since \(W\subseteq V\text{,}\) and since \(\dim V=n\text{,}\) the linearly independent tuples \(S_i\) must satisfy \(i=\len S_i\leq n\text{.}\) Thus the procedure must halt with a basis \(B\) of \(W\) satisfying \(\len B\leq n\text{.}\) We conclude that \(\dim W\leq \dim V\text{.}\)
  2. Clearly, if \(W=V\text{,}\) then \(\dim W=\dim V\text{.}\) For the other direction, assume \(\dim W=\dim V=n\text{.}\) Let \(B\) be a basis for \(W\text{.}\) Since \(B\) is linearly independent, it can be extended to a basis \(B'\) of \(V\) (1.8.8). Since \(\len B=\dim W=\dim V\text{,}\) and since all bases of \(V\) have cardinality \(n\text{,}\) we must have \(B=B'\text{.}\) It follows that \(B\) is also a basis of \(V\text{,}\) and hence that
    \begin{equation*} V=\Span B=W\text{.} \end{equation*}
