Corollary 1.6.14. Span and independence.

Let \(S=(v_1,v_2,\dots, v_n)\) be an \(n\)-tuple of vectors of the vector space \(V\text{.}\) The following statements are equivalent.
  1. \(S\) is linearly dependent.
  2. There exists \(k\in \{1,2,\dots, n\}\) such that
    \begin{equation*} v_k=c_1v_1+c_2v_2+\cdots c_{k-1}v_{k-1}\text{.} \end{equation*}
    for some \(c_i\in F\text{.}\) Furthermore, when this is the case we have
    \begin{equation} \Span(v_1,v_2,\dots, v_n)=\Span(v_1,\dots, v_{k-1},v_{k+1},\dots, v_n)\text{.}\tag{1.17} \end{equation}
