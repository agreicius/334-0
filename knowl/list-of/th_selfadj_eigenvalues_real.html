Theorem 1.23.9. Eigenvalues of self-adjoint operators.

Let \(F\in \{\R, \C\}\text{,}\) let \((V,\angvec{\, ,})\) be a finite-dimensional inner product space over \(F\text{,}\) and let \(T\in \mathcal{L}(V)\) be a self-adjoint operator.
  1. If \(\lambda\) is an eigenvalue \(T\text{,}\) then \(\lambda\in \R\text{.}\) In other words, all eigenvalues of \(T\) are real.
  2. The characteristic polynomial \(f\) of \(T\) factors as
    \begin{equation*} f(x)=(x-\lambda_1)^{n_1}(x-\lambda_2)^{n_2}\cdots (x-\lambda_r)^{n_r}\text{,} \end{equation*}
    where \(\lambda_i\ne \lambda_j\) for all \(i\ne j\text{,}\) and \(\lambda_i\in \R\) for all \(1\leq i\leq r\text{.}\)

Proof.
