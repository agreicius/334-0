Definition 1.17.1. Diagonalizable.

Let \(V\) be a finite-dimensional vector space. A linear operator \(T\in \mathcal{L}(V)\) is diagonalizable if there exists a basis \(B\) of \(V\) such that \([T]_B\) is a diagonal matrix. In this case, we say that the basis \(B\) diagonalizes \(T\text{.}\)
