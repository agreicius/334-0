Corollary 1.8.13. Existence of direct sums.

Assume \(V\) is a finite-dimensional vector space. Given any subspace \(W\subseteq V\) there is a subpace \(W'\subseteq V\) satisfying \(V=W\oplus W'\text{.}\)

Proof.

Assume \(\dim V=n\text{.}\) Given a subspace \(W\subset V\text{,}\) we know that \(\dim W\leq n\text{,}\) and hence that \(W\) has a finite basis \(S=(v_1,v_2,\dots, v_r)\) for some \(r\leq n\text{.}\) Since \(S\) is linearly independent, it can be extended to a basis \(B=(v_1,v_2,\dots, v_r,v_{r+1},\cdots, v_n)\) of \(V\text{.}\) Define \(W'=\Span(v_{r+1}, \dots, v_n)\text{.}\) We claim that \(W\oplus W'=V\text{.}\)
