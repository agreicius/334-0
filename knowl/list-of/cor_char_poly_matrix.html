Corollary 1.16.8. Characteristic polynomial of a matrix.

Let \(T\in\mathcal{L}(V)\text{,}\) where \(\dim V=n< \infty\text{,}\) and let \(f(x)=x^n+a_{n-1}x+\cdots +a_1x+a_0\) be the characteristic polynomial of \(T\text{.}\)
  1. The polynomial \(f\) can be factored over \(F\) as
    \begin{align} f(x) \amp =(x-\lambda_1)^{n_1}(x-\lambda_2)^{n_2}\cdots (x-\lambda_r)^{n_r}g(x)\text{,}\tag{1.48} \end{align}
    where \(\lambda_i\in F\) for all \(1\leq i\leq r\text{,}\) \(\lambda_i\ne \lambda_j\) for \(i\ne j\text{,}\) and \(g(x)\in P(F)\) is a polynomial with no roots in \(F\text{.}\) It follows that \(\lambda_1,\lambda_2,\dots, \lambda_r\) are the distinct eigenvalues of \(T_A\text{.}\)
  2. \(T_A\) has at most \(n\) distinct eigenvalues.
