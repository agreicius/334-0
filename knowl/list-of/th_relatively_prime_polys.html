Theorem 1.25.5. Relatively prime polynomials.

Let \(F\) be a field, and let \(f_1,f_2,\dots, f_r\) be a relatively prime list of polynomials. There are polynomials \(g_1,g_2,\dots, g_r\) such that
\begin{equation} 1=g_1(x)f_1(x)+g_2(x)f_2(x)+\cdots +g_r(x)f_r(x)\text{.}\tag{1.127} \end{equation}

Proof.

The proof, though not difficult, lies somewhat outside the scope of this course. This and other results about the theory of polynomials is covered in the ring theory portion of a course in abstract algebra. Moreover, it is an instance of a more general result involving the greatest common divisor of the polynomials \(f_1,f_2,\dots, f_r\text{.}\)
