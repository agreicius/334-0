Definition 1.24.7. Multiplicity of eigenvalues.

Let \(T\in \mathcal{L}(V)\text{,}\) where \(V\) is finite dimensional, let \(f\in P(F)\) be the characteristic polynomial of \(T\text{,}\) and let \(\lambda\) be an eigenvalue of \(T\text{.}\)
  • Algebraic multiplicity.
    The algebraic multiplicity of \(\lambda\) is its multiplicity as a root of \(f\text{:}\) i.e. the algebraic multiplicity of \(\lambda\) is the positive integer \(m\) such that \(f(x)=(x-\lambda)^mg(x)\) for some \(g\in P(F)\) satisfying \(g(\lambda)\ne 0\text{.}\)
  • Geometric multiplicity.
    The geometric multiplicity of \(\lambda\) is defined as \(\dim E(\lambda, T)\text{.}\)
