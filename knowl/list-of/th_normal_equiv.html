Theorem 1.22.9. Normality equivalence.

Let \(T\) be a linear operator on the inner product space \((V,\angvec{\, ,})\text{.}\) The following statements are equivalent.
  1. \(T\) is normal.
  2. \(\norm{T(v)}=\norm{T^*(v)}\) for all \(v\in V\text{.}\)
  3. \(\angvec{T(v),T(w)}=\angvec{T^*(v),T^*(w)}\) for all \(v\in V\text{.}\)

Proof.

We prove a cycle of implications.
If \(T\) is normal, then
\begin{align*} \norm{T(v)}^2 \amp =\angvec{T(v),T(v)}\\ \amp =\angvec{v,T^*Tv}\\ \amp =\angvec{v,TT^*v} \amp (T^*T=TT^*)\\ \amp =\angvec{T^*(v),T^*(v)}\\ \amp =\norm{T^*(v)}^2\text{.} \end{align*}
Taking square-roots of both sides (and using the fact that norms are nonnegative), we conclude that \(\norm{T(v)}=\norm{T^*(v)}\text{.}\) This proves (1)\(\implies\)(2).
To prove (2)\(\implies\)(3), we use the polarity identity. If \(\norm{T(v)}=\norm{T^*(v)}\) for all \(v\in V\text{,}\) then we have
\begin{align*} \angvec{T(v),T(w)} \amp = \frac{1}{4}(\norm{T(v)+T(w)}^2-\norm{T(v)-T(w)}^2)+\frac{1}{4}(\norm{T(v)+iT(w)}^2-\norm{T(v)-iT(w)}^2)i\\ \amp =\frac{1}{4}(\norm{(T(v+w))}^2-\norm{T(v-w)}^2)+\frac{1}{4}(\norm{T(v+iw)}^2-\norm{T(v-iw)}^2)i\\ \amp = \frac{1}{4}(\norm{(T^*(v+w))}^2-\norm{T^*(v-w)}^2)+\frac{1}{4}(\norm{T^*(v+iw)}^2-\norm{T^*(v-iw)}^2)i\\ \amp =\frac{1}{4}(\norm{T^*(v)+T^*(w)}^2-\norm{T^*(v)-T^*(w)}^2)+\frac{1}{4}(\norm{T^*(v)+\overline{i}T^*(w)}^2-\norm{T^*(v)-\overline{i}T^*(w)}^2)i \\ \amp = \angvec{T^*(v),T^*(w)} \end{align*}
