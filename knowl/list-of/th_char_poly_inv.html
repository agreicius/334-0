Theorem 1.24.5. Invariance and characteristic polynomial.

Let \(T\in \mathcal{L}(V)\text{,}\) where \(V\) is a finite-dimensional vector space, and let \(W\subseteq V\) be a \(T\)-invariant subspace. If \(f\) is the characteristic polynomial of \(T\) and \(g\) is the characteristic polynomial of \(T\vert_W\text{,}\) then \(g\mid f\text{.}\) In other words, we have
\begin{equation*} f(x)=q(x)g(x) \end{equation*}
for some \(q(x)\in P(F)\text{.}\)

Proof.
