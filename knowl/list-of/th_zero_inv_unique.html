Theorem 1.2.17. Uniqueness of zero vector and inverses.

Let \(V\) be an \(F\)-vector space.
  1. The zero vector in \(V\) is unique: i.e., there is one and only one element of \(V\) that satisfies (1.3).
  2. Inverse vectors are unique: i.e., for all \(\boldv\in V\) there is one and only one vector \(-\boldv\) satisfying (1.4).
