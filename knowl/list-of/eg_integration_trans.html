Example 1.9.17. Integration.

Let \([a,b]\) be a closed interval of \(\R\text{.}\) Prove that the function
\begin{align*} T\colon C([a,b]) \amp\rightarrow \R \\ f \amp \longmapsto \int_a^bf\, dx \end{align*}
is a linear transformation.
Solution.
Similarly to the above examples, the linearity of \(T\) follows from familiar properties of the integral: for all \(f,g\in C([a,b])\) and \(c,d\in \R\text{,}\) we have
\begin{align*} T(cf+dg) \amp =\int_a^b cf+dg\, dx\\ \amp =c\int_a^bf\, dx+d\int_a^bg\, dx\\ \amp =cT(f)+dT(g)\text{.} \end{align*}
