Procedure 1.16.13. Computing eigenspaces of operators.

Let \(T\in\mathcal{L}(V)\text{,}\) where \(\dim V=n< \infty\text{.}\) To compute the eigenvalues and eigenspaces of \(T\text{,}\) proceed as follows.
  1. Pick any basis \(B\) of \(V\) and compute \(A=[T]_B\text{.}\)
  2. Compute \(f(x)=\det(xI-A)\text{,}\) the characteristic polynomial of \(T\) and factor \(f\) as in (1.48) to find the distinct eigenvalues \(\lambda_1,\lambda_2,\dots, \lambda_r\) of \(T\text{.}\)
  3. For each \(1\leq i\leq r\) compute a basis \(C_i\) of \(E(\lambda_i, T_A)=\NS(\lambda I-A)\) using the null space algorithm for matrices.
  4. For each \(1\leq i\leq n\text{,}\) “lift” the basis \(C_i\) of \(E(\lambda_i, T_A)\) to a basis \(B_i\) of \(E(\lambda_i, T)\) using the inverse coordinate map \([\phantom{v}]_B^{-1}\text{.}\)
