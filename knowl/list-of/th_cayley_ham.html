Theorem 1.24.6. Cayley-Hamilton theorem.

Let \(T\in\mathcal{L}(V)\text{,}\) where \(V\) is finite dimensional, and let \(f\in P(F)\) be the characteristic polynomial of \(T\text{.}\) We have
\begin{equation} f(T)=0_V\text{.}\tag{1.121} \end{equation}
In other words, \(T\) is a “root” of its own characteristic polynomial.

Proof.
