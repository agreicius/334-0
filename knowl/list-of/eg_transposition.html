Example 1.9.15. Matrix transpose.

Define \(S\colon F^{m,n}\rightarrow F^{n,m}\) as \(S(A)=A^T\text{:}\) i.e., \(S\) is the transpose operation. Prove that \(S\) is a linear transformation.
Solution.
The linearity of \(S\) follows from elementary properties of the transpose operator. Using the one-step technique, for any \(A,A'\in F^{m,n}\) and scalars \(c,d\in F\text{,}\) we have
\begin{align*} S(cA+dA') \amp =(cA+dA')^T\\ \amp =cA^T+d(A')^T \amp (\text{prop. of transp.})\\ \amp =cS(A)+dS(A')\text{.} \end{align*}
This proves \(S\) is linear.
