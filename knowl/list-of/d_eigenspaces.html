Definition 1.15.13. Eigenspaces.

Let \(T\in \mathcal{L}(V)\text{.}\) For all \(\lambda\in F\) we define the \(\lambda\)-eigenspace of \(T\), denoted \(E(\lambda, T)\text{,}\) as
\begin{equation} E(\lambda, T)=\NS(\lambda I_V-T)\text{.}\tag{1.46} \end{equation}
Note that the nonzero elements of \(E(\lambda, T)\) are precisely the \(\lambda\)-eigenvectors of \(T\text{,}\) and in particular, that \(\lambda\) is an eigenvalue of \(T\) if and only if \(E(\lambda, T)\) is a nonzero subspace of \(V\text{.}\)
