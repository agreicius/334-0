Example 1.21.14. Adjoint of inclusion map.

Let \(W\) be a finite-dimensional subspace of the inner product space \((V,\angvec{\, })\text{,}\) and let \(i\colon W\rightarrow V\) be the inclusion map. Given an explicit description of the adjoint map \(i^*\colon V\rightarrow W\text{.}\)
Solution.
Since \(W\) is finite dimensional, we have \(V=W\oplus W^\perp\text{,}\) and can define the orthogonal projection map \(\proj_W\colon V\rightarrow W\text{.}\) We claim that \(i^*=\proj_W\text{,}\) and prove our claim by showing that
\begin{equation*} \angvec{i(w'),v}=\angvec{w,\proj_W(v)} \end{equation*}
for all \(w'\in W\) and \(v\in V\text{.}\) Given any \(v\in V\text{,}\) we write \(v=w+w^\perp\text{,}\) where by definition \(w=\proj_W(v)\in W\) and \(w^\perp\in W^\perp\text{.}\) It follows that for any \(w'\in W\text{,}\) we have
\begin{align*} \angvec{i(w'),v} \amp =\angvec{w',v}\\ \amp = \angvec{w',w+w^\perp}\\ \amp =\angvec{w',w}+\angvec{w',w^\perp}\\ \amp =\angvec{w',w} \amp (\angvec{w',w^\perp}=0)\\ \amp = \angvec{w',\proj_W(v)}\text{,} \end{align*}
as desired.
