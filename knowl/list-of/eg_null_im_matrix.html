Example 1.10.9. Matrix transformation.

Let \(A\) be an \(m\times n\) matrix, and let \(\bolda_1,\bolda_2,\dots, \bolda_n\) be the \(n\) columns of \(A\text{,}\) considered as elements of \(F^m\text{.}\) The null space and image of the corresponding matrix transformation \(T_A\colon F^n\rightarrow F^m\) can be identified with the following fundamental spaces of \(A\text{:}\)
\begin{align*} \NS T_A \amp = \NS A=\{\boldx\in F^n\colon A\boldx=\boldzero\}\\ \im T_A \amp =\CS A=\Span(\bolda_1,\bolda_2,\dots, \bolda_n)\text{.} \end{align*}
