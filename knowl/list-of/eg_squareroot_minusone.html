Example 1.1.19. \(i^2\).

Compute \(i^2\text{.}\)
Solution.
We have, using the definition of multiplication,
\begin{equation*} i^2=(0+1\, i)(0+1\, i)=(0-1)+0i=-1\text{.} \end{equation*}
Thus the element \(i\) is seen to be a square-root of \(-1\text{!}\) With a little more work, we can show that any complex number \(z=a+ib\) has a complex square-root. More on that later.
