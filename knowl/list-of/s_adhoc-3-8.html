Example 1.19.8.

Consider the inner product space \(V=\R^2\) with the dot product.
  1. Verify that \(B'=(\boldx_1=(\sqrt{3}/2,1/2), \boldx_2=(-1/2,\sqrt{3}/2))\) is an orthonormal basis of \(\R^2\text{.}\)
  2. Let \(\boldx=(4,2)\text{.}\) Find the scalars \(c_1, c_2\in \R\) such that \(\boldx=c_1\boldx_1+c_2\boldx_2\text{.}\)
  3. Verify that \(\norm{\boldx}=\sqrt{c_1^2+c_2^2}\text{.}\)
Solution.
  1. Easily verified.
  2. Using TheoremÂ 1.19.7 we compute
    \begin{align*} c_1 \amp =\boldx\cdot \boldx_1=2\sqrt{3}+1 \\ c_2\amp= \boldx\cdot \boldx_2=\sqrt{3}-2 \text{.} \end{align*}
  3. Computing directly yields \(\norm{\boldx}=\sqrt{20}=2\sqrt{5}\text{.}\) Using the generalized Pythagorean theorem we have
    \begin{align*} \norm{\boldx} \amp= \sqrt{(2\sqrt{3}+1)^2+(\sqrt{3}-2)^2} \\ \amp=\sqrt{(12+4\sqrt{3}+1)+(3-4\sqrt{3}+4)} \\ \amp = \sqrt{20}=2\sqrt{5}\text{,} \end{align*}
    as desired.
