Theorem 1.25.2. Generalized eigenspaces.

Let \(T\in \mathcal{L}(V)\text{,}\) where \(V\) is finite dimensional.
  1. For all \(\lambda\in F\text{,}\) \(G(\lambda, T)\) is a \(T\)-invariant subspace and \(E(\lambda, T)\subseteq G(\lambda, T)\text{.}\)
  2. \(G(\lambda, T)\cap G(\lambda', T)=\{\boldzero\}\) for \(\lambda\be \lambda'\in F\text{.}\)
  3. Let \(\lambda\) be an eigenvalue of \(T\) of algebraic multiplicty \(m\text{.}\) We have
    \begin{equation} 1\leq \dim E(\lamdba, T)\leq \dim G(\lambda, T)\leq m\tag{1.124} \end{equation}
    and
    \begin{equation} G(\lambda, T)=\NS(T-\lambda I)^m\text{.}\tag{1.125} \end{equation}

Proof.
