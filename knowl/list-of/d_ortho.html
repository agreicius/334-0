Definition 1.19.1. Orthogonality.

Let \((V,\angvec{\, })\) be an inner product space.
  • Orthogonal vectors.
    Vectors \(v,w\in V\) are orthogonal if \(\angvec{v,w}=0\text{.}\)
  • Orthogonal tuple.
    Let \(S=(v_i)_{i\in I}\) be a tuple of nonzero vectors of \(V\text{.}\) We say \(S\) is orthogonal if \(\angvec{v_i,v_j}=0\) for all \(i,j\in I\) with \(i\ne j\text{:}\) i.e., the vectors of \(S\) are pairwise orthogonal. We say \(S\) is orthonormal if it is orthogonal and \(\norm{v_i}=1\) for all \(i\in I\text{.}\)
