Theorem 1.8.13. Direct sums.

Assume \(V\) is a finite-dimensional vector space.
  1. Given any subspace \(W\subseteq V\) there is a subpace \(W'\subseteq V\) satisfying \(V=W\oplus W'\text{.}\)
  2. Let \(W=\sum_{i=1}^r W_i\text{,}\) where \(W_i\) is a subspace of \(V\) for all \(1\leq i\leq r\text{.}\) The following statements are equivalent.
    1. \(W=\bigoplus_{i=1}^r W_i\text{.}\)
    2. \(\dim W=\sum_{i=1}^r\dim W_i\text{.}\)
Given any subspace \(W\subseteq V\) there is a subpace \(W'\subseteq V\) satisfying \(V=W\oplus W'\text{.}\)

Proof.

Assume \(\dim V=n\text{.}\) Given a subspace \(W\subset V\text{,}\) we know that \(\dim W\leq n\text{,}\) and hence that \(W\) has a finite basis \(S=(v_1,v_2,\dots, v_r)\) for some \(r\leq n\text{.}\) Since \(S\) is linearly independent, it can be extended to a basis \(B=(v_1,v_2,\dots, v_r,v_{r+1},\cdots, v_n)\) of \(V\text{.}\) Define \(W'=\Span(v_{r+1}, \dots, v_n)\text{.}\) We claim that \(W\oplus W'=V\text{.}\)
