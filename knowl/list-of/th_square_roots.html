Theorem 1.26.10. Square-roots of matrices.

Let \(A\in F^{n,n}\) be an invertible matrix whose characteristic polynomial splits completely over \(F\text{.}\) There is a matrix \(B\) such that \(B^2=A\text{.}\) In other words, if \(A\) is invertible and the characteristic polynomial of \(A\) splits completely over \(F\text{,}\) then \(A\) has a square root.
