Theorem 1.25.6. Factorization of polynomials.

Let \(F\) be a field, and let \(f\in P(F)\) be a nonzero polynomial.
  1. Irreducible factorization.
    We can factor \(f\) in the form
    \begin{equation} f(x)=cf_1(x)^{n_1}f_2(x)^{n_2}\cdots f_r^{n_r}(x)\text{,}\tag{1.128} \end{equation}
    where the factors \(f_i\) are pairwise relatively prime, monic irreducible polynomials.
  2. Uniqueness of factorization.
    The scalar \(c\text{,}\) set of monic irreducible polynomials \(\{f_1,f_2,\dots, f_r\}\) and powers \(n_i\) are all uniquely determined by \(f\text{.}\)

Proof.

The proof, though not difficult, lies somewhat outside the scope of this course. This and other results about the theory of polynomials is covered in the ring theory portion of a course in abstract algebra.
