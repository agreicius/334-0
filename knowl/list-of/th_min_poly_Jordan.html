Theorem 1.26.8. Minimal polynomial from Jordan form.

Let \(T\in \mathcal{L}(V)\text{,}\) where \(V\) is finite dimensional, and suppose the characteristic polynomial \(f\) of \(T\) factors as
\begin{equation*} f(x)=\prod_{i=1}^r(x-\lambda_i)^{n_i}\text{,} \end{equation*}
where \(\lambda_i\ne \lambda_j\) for \(i\ne j\text{,}\) and \(\lambda_i\in F\text{.}\) Let \(m_T(x)\) be the minimal polynomial of \(T\text{.}\) We have
\begin{equation*} m_T(x)=\prod_{i=1}^r(x-\lambda_i)^{m_i}\text{,} \end{equation*}
where \(m_i\) is the maximum size of a \(\lambda_i\)-Jordan block appearing in a Jordan form for \(T\text{.}\)

Proof.
