Definition 1.26.1. Jordan block.

Let \(F\) be a field. Given \(\lambda\in F\) and positive integer \(k\text{,}\) the \(k\times k\) matrix
\begin{equation*} J=\begin{bmatrix} \lambda \amp 1\amp 0 \amp \dots \amp 0\\ 0 \amp \lambda\amp 1 \amp \\ \vdots \amp \amp \ddots \amp \ddots \\ \amp \amp \amp \amp 1\\ 0\amp \dots \amp \amp \amp \lambda \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
is called a \(\lambda\)-Jordan block.
