Example 1.17.9. Matrix power.

Let \(A=\begin{bmatrix} 3\amp 1\\ 1\amp 3 \end{bmatrix}\text{.}\) Compute \(A^{100}\)
Solution.
The characteristic polynomial of \(A\) is \(f(x)=x^2-6x+8=(x-4)(x-2)\text{.}\) The usual computation shows that
\begin{align*} E(2, T_A) \amp = \Span ((1,-1)) \\ E(4,T_A) \amp =\Span ((1,1))\text{,} \end{align*}
and thus that we have \(D=P^{-1}AP\text{,}\) where
\begin{equation*} D=\begin{bmatrix} 2\amp 0 \\ 0\amp 4 \end{bmatrix}, P=\begin{bmatrix} 1\amp 1 \\ -1\amp 1 \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}
From the discussion above, we conclude that
\begin{align*} A^{100} \amp =(PDP^{-1})^{100}\\ \amp = PD^{100}P^{-1}\\ \amp =\begin{bmatrix} 1\amp 1 \\ -1\amp 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2^{100}\amp 0 \\ 0\amp 4^{100} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1/2\amp -1/2 \\ 1/2\amp 1/2 \end{bmatrix}\\ \amp = \begin{bmatrix} 1\amp 1 \\ -1\amp 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2^{99}\amp -2^{99} \\ 2^{199}\amp 2^{199} \end{bmatrix}\\ \amp = \begin{bmatrix} 2^{199}+2^{99} \amp 2^{199}-2^{99} \\ 2^{199}-2^{99} \amp 2^{199}+2^{99} \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{align*}
