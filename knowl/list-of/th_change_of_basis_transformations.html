Theorem 1.14.11. Change of basis for transformations.

Let \(V\) be finite-dimensional, let \(T\colon V\rightarrow V\) be linear, and let \(B\) and \(B'\) be two ordered bases for \(V\text{.}\) We have
\begin{equation} [T]_{B'}=\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\, [T]_B\, \underset{B'\rightarrow B}{P}\text{,}\tag{1.41} \end{equation}
or equivalently
\begin{equation} [T]_{B'}=\underset{B'\rightarrow B}{P}^{-1}\, [T]_B\, \underset{B'\rightarrow B}{P}\text{.}\tag{1.42} \end{equation}

Proof.

First observe that (1.42) follows from (1.41) and (3) of Theorem 1.14.2. Next, to prove (1.41), it suffices by (2) of Theorem 1.13.14 to show that the matrix \(A=\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\, [T]_B\, \underset{B'\rightarrow B}{P}\) satisfies
\begin{equation*} A[v]_{B'}=[T(v)]_{B'} \end{equation*}
for all \(v\in V\text{.}\) To this end, given any \(v\in V\text{,}\) we have
\begin{align*} A[v]_{B'}=\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\, [T]_B\, \underset{B'\rightarrow B}{P}[v]_{B'} \amp= \underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\, [T]_B [v]_B \amp (\knowl{./knowl/xref/th_change_basis_props.html}{\text{Theorem 1.14.2}})\\ \amp= \underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}[T(v)]_{B} \amp (\knowl{./knowl/xref/th_matrixrep.html}{\text{Theorem 1.13.14}}, (1)) \\ \amp = [v]_{B'} \amp (\knowl{./knowl/xref/th_change_basis_props.html}{\text{Theorem 1.14.2}})\text{.} \end{align*}
