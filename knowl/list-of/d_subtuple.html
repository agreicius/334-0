Definition 1.7.17. Subtuples.

Let \((a_i)_{i\in I}\) be a tuple of elements of the set \(A\text{.}\) Given a subset \(J\subseteq I\text{,}\) we call the tuple \((a_j)_{j\in J}\) a subtuple of \((a_i)_{i\in I}\text{.}\)
Thinking in terms of functions, if \((a_i)_{i\in I}\) is the tuple representation of the function \(f\colon I\rightarrow A\text{,}\) then the subtuple \((a_j)_{j\in J}\) is the tuple representation of the restriction \(f\vert_J\colon J\rightarrow A\) of \(f\) to \(J\text{.}\)
