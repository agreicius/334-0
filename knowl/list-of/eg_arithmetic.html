Example 1.1.21. Complex arithmetic.

Let \(z=-2+3i\text{,}\) \(w=4+i\text{.}\) Compute the following complex numbers. Your answer must be expressed in the form \(a+ib\text{,}\) where \(a,b\in \R\text{.}\)
  1. \(\displaystyle z+w\)
  2. \(\displaystyle z-w\)
  3. \(\displaystyle z\,w\)
  4. \(\displaystyle z/w\)
Solution.
  1. We have
    \begin{align*} z+w \amp =(-2+4)+i(3+1)\\ \amp = 2+i\,4\text{.} \end{align*}
  2. We have
    \begin{align*} z-w \amp = (-2-4)+(3-1)i \\ \amp = -6+i\,2\text{.} \end{align*}
  3. We have
    \begin{align*} z\,w \amp = (-2\cdot 4-3\cdot 1)+i(-2\cdot 1+3\cdot 4)\\ \amp = -11+i\,10\text{.} \end{align*}
  4. First observe that by definition we have
    \begin{equation*} z/w=z\, w^{-1}\text{.} \end{equation*}
    According to the inverse formulas in TheoremÂ 1.1.17 we have
    \begin{equation*} w^{-1}=\frac{4}{4^2+1^2}-i\frac{1}{4^2+1^1}=\frac{1}{17}(4-i)\text{.} \end{equation*}
    We thus have
    \begin{align*} z/w \amp = z\, w^{-1}\\ \amp = w^{-1}\, z\\ \amp = \frac{1}{17}(4-i)(-2+3i)\\ \amp = \frac{1}{17}(-5+14i)\\ \amp = -\frac{5}{17}+\frac{14}{17}i\text{.} \end{align*}
