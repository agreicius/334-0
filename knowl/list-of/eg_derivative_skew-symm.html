Example 1.22.5. Derivative operator.

Let \(W\) be the subspace of real periodic \(C^\infty\) functions on \(\R\) with period \(1\text{.}\) Recall that the derivative operator \(T\in \mathcal{L}(W)\) defined as \(T(f)=f'\) has adjoint \(T^*-T\) with respect to the inner product \(\angvec{f,g}=\int_0^1f(x)g(x)\, dx\text{.}\) Hence \(T\) is skew-symmetric. In particular, \(T\) is normal.
