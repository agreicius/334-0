Example 1.9.19. Shift transformations.

Prove that the functions \(T_\ell\) and \(T_r\) defined as
\begin{align*} T_\ell\colon F^\infty \amp \rightarrow F^\infty \amp T_r\colon F^\infty\amp \rightarrow F^\infty\\ (a_1,a_2,a_3,\dots) \amp \longmapsto (a_2,a_3,\dots) \amp (a_1,a_2,\dots)\amp \mapsto (0,a_1,a_2,\dots) \end{align*}
are linear transformations.
Solution.
We prove that \(T_\ell\) is a linear transformation, leaving the proof for \(T_r\) to the reader. Using tuple notation, we have \(T_\ell((a_n)_{n=1}^\infty)=(a_{n+1})_{n=1}^\infty\text{.}\) Thus, for any tuples \((a_n)_{n=1}^\infty, (b_n)_{n=1}^\infty\in F^\infty\) and scalars \(c,d\in F\text{,}\) we have
\begin{align*} T_\ell(c(a_n)_{n=1}^\infty+d(b_n)_{n=1}^\infty) \amp = T_\ell(ca_n+db_n)_{n=1}^\infty\\ \amp =(ca_{n+1}+db_{n+1})_{n=1}^{\infty}\\ \amp =c(a_{n+1})_{n=1}^\infty+d(b_{n+1})_{n=1}^\infty\\ \amp =cT_\ell((a_n)_{n=1}^\infty)+dT((b_n)_{n=1}^\infty)\text{.} \end{align*}
