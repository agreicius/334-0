Example 1.20.15.

Consider the plane \(W=\{(x,y,z)\in\R^3\colon x-2y+z=0-\}\text{.}\)
  1. Find the matrix \(A\) such that \(\operatorname{proj}_W=T_A\text{.}\)
  2. Use your matrix formula from (a) to compute \(\proj_W(2,1,1)\) and \(\proj_W(1,1,1)\text{.}\)
  3. Compute \(d((2,1,1),W)\) and \(d((1,1,1), W)\text{.}\)
Solution.
  1. Using the general formula described in ExampleÂ 1.20.14, we have
    \begin{equation*} A=\frac{1}{6}\begin{amatrix}[rrr] 5\amp 2\amp -1\\ 2\amp 2\amp 2\\ -1\amp 2\amp 5 \end{amatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}
  2. Now compute
    \begin{align*} \proj_W(2,1,1)\amp= \frac{1}{6}\begin{amatrix}[rrr] 5\amp 2\amp -1\\ 2\amp 2\amp 2\\ -1\amp 2\amp 5 \end{amatrix} \begin{amatrix}[r] 2 \\ 1\\ 1 \end{amatrix}= \frac{1}{6}\begin{amatrix}[r] 11\\ 8\\ 5 \end{amatrix}\\ \proj_W(1,1,1)\amp= \frac{1}{6}\begin{amatrix}[rrr] 5\amp 2\amp -1\\ 2\amp 2\amp 2\\ -1\amp 2\amp 5 \end{amatrix} \begin{amatrix}[r] 1 \\ 1\\ 1 \end{amatrix}= \frac{1}{6}\begin{amatrix}[r] 0\\ 0\\ 0 \end{amatrix}\text{.} \end{align*}
  3. We have
    \begin{align*} d((2,1,1), W) \amp = \norm{(2,1,1)-\proj_W(2,1,1)}\\ \amp =\norm{\frac{1}{6}(1,-2,-1)}=\frac{6}{6} \\ d((1,1,1), W) \amp = \norm{(1,1,1)-\proj_W(1,1,1)}\\ \amp =\norm{(0,0,0)}=0 \text{.} \end{align*}
