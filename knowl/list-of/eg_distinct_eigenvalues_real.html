Example 1.17.16. Real linear operator.

Assume \(T\) is a linear operator of the 3-dimensional \(\R\)-vector space \(V\text{,}\) that the characteristic polynomial of \(T\) is \(f(x)=x^3-1\text{,}\) and that \(T\ne I_V\text{.}\) Decide whether \(T\) is diagonalizable.
Solution.
Over \(\R\) the characteristic polynomial \(f\) factors as
\begin{equation*} f(x)=(x-1)(x^2+x+1)\text{.} \end{equation*}
Thus \(1\) is the only eigenvalue of \(T\text{.}\) If \(T\) were diagonalizable, we would have
\begin{equation*} V=\bigoplus E(\lambda_i, T)=E(1,T)\text{.} \end{equation*}
But then \(v\in E(1,T)\) for all \(v\in V\text{,}\) in which case
\begin{equation*} T(v)=1v=v \end{equation*}
for all \(v\in V\text{:}\) i.e., we would have \(T=I_V\) in this case. We conclude that \(T\) is not diagonalizable.
