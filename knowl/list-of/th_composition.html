Theorem 1.12.2. Composition of linear transformations.

Let \(T,T_1,T_2\in \mathcal{L}(U,V)\text{,}\) \(S,S_1,S_2\in \mathcal{L}(V,W)\text{,}\) and \(R\in \mathcal{L}(W,W')\text{.}\)
  1. The composition \(ST\colon U\rightarrow W\) is a linear transformation.
  2. \(\displaystyle R(ST)=(RS)T\)
  3. \(S(T_1+T_2)=ST_1+ST_1\) and \((S_1+S_2)T=S_1T+S_2T\text{.}\)
  4. \(TI_U=T\) and \(I_VT=T\text{.}\)

Proof.

The proofs of (2)-(4) follow pretty much directly from the definitions of the function operations involved and basic set-theoretic properties of functions and their compositions. As such we content ourselves with a proof only of (1). Assume \(T\in\mathcal{L}(U,V)\) and \(S\in \mathcal{L}(V,W)\text{.}\) We use the one-step technique to show the composition \(ST\colon U\rightarrow W\) is linear. Given any \(v,v'\in V\) and scalars \(c,d\in F\text{,}\) we have
\begin{align*} ST(cv+dv') \amp = S(T(cv+dv')) \amp (\text{def. of comp.})\\ \amp =S(cT(v)+dT(v')) \amp (T \text{ linear}) \\ \amp = cS(T(v))+dS(T(v')) \amp (S \text{ linear})\\ \amp =cST(v)+dST(v')\text{.} \end{align*}
