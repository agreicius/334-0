Example 1.12.8. Evaluation.

Let \(I\) be an infinite subset of the field \(F\text{,}\) let \(n\) be a positive integer, and let \(\boldc=(c_1,c_2,\dots, c_{n+1})\in F^{n+1}\) be a choice of \(n+1\) distinct scalars (i.e., \(c_i\ne c_j\) for \(i\ne j\)). Prove that the evaluation map
\begin{align*} T\colon P_n(I) \amp \rightarrow F^{n+1}\\ f \amp \longmapsto (f(c_1),f(c_2),\dots, f(c_{n+1})) \end{align*}
is an isomorphism.
Solution.
We showed in ExampleÂ 1.10.8 that this linear transformation is bijective, and hence an isomorphism.
