Theorem 1.2.11. Function spaces are vector spaces.

Each set and accompanying vector operations in Definition 1.2.7–1.2.10 forms a vector space.

Proof.

We follow Procedure 1.2.5. The definition has taken care of steps (1)-(3) for us, it remains to show that the vector space axioms are satisfied. As you will see, the tuple notation for functions makes these proofs quite straightforward consequences of the field axioms satisfied by \(F\text{.}\)
Consider the arithmetic axioms first. We will verify that Axioms (i), (ii), and (v) are satisfied. Given \(f=(f(i))_{i\in I},g=(g(i))_{i\in I},h=(h(i))_{i\in I}\in F^I\) and \(c\in F\text{,}\) we have
\begin{align*} f+g \amp = (f(i)+g(i))_{i\in I} \amp (\text{def. vec. add.}) \\ \amp = (g(i)+f(i))_{i\in I} \amp (\text{field prop.})\\ \amp = g+f \amp (\text{def. vec. add.})\\ f+(g+h) \amp = (f(i)+(g+h)(i))_{i\in I} \amp (\text{def. vec. add.})\\ \amp = (f(i)+(g(i)+h(i)))_{i\in I} \amp (\text{def. vec. add.})\\ \amp = ((f(i)+g(i))+h(i))_{i\in I} \amp (\text{field prop.})\\ \amp = ((f+g)(i)+h(i))_{i\in I} \amp (\text{def. vec. add.})\\ \amp = (f+g)+h \amp (\text{def. vec. add.})\\ c(f+g) \amp =(c(f+g)(i))_{i\in I} \amp (\text{def. scal. mult})\\ \amp = (c(f(i)+g(i)))_{i\in I} \amp (\text{def. vec. add.})\\ \amp = (cf(i)+cg(i))_{i\in I} \amp (\text{field prop.})\\ \amp =((cf)(i)+(cg)(i))_{i\in I} \amp (\text{def. scal. mult})\\ \amp = cf+cg \amp (\text{def. vec. add.})\text{.} \end{align*}
Lastly we will show that \(0_I\) does indeed satisfy the additive identity axiom (Axiom (iii)), leaving the verification of Axiom (iv) to the reader. Given any element \(f=(f(i))_{i\in I}\in F^I\text{,}\) we have
\begin{align*} 0_I+f \amp =(0_I(i)+f(i))_{i\in I} \amp (\text{def. vec. add.})\\ \amp = (0+f(i))_{i\in I}\amp (\text{def. of } 0_I)\\ \amp = (f(i))_{i\in I} \amp (\text{field prop.})\\ \amp = f\text{,} \end{align*}
as desired.
