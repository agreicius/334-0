Theorem 1.19.7. Calculating with orthogonal bases.

Let \((V,\angvec{\, ,})\) be an inner product space of dimension \(n\text{.}\)
  1. Let \(B=(v_1,v_2,\dots, v_n)\) be an orthogonal basis of \(V\text{.}\) For any \(v\in V\) we have
    \begin{equation*} v=c_1v_1+c_2v_2+\cdots +c_nv_n\text{,} \end{equation*}
    where
    \begin{equation} c_i=\frac{\langle v,v_i\rangle}{\langle v_i,v_i\rangle}\tag{1.68} \end{equation}
    for all \(1\leq i\leq n\text{.}\)
    If \(B\) is orthonormal, so that \(\langle v_i, v_i\rangle =1\) for all \(1\leq i\leq n\text{,}\) then the inner product formula (1.68) reduces to the simpler
    \begin{equation} c_i=\langle v, v_i\rangle\text{.}\tag{1.69} \end{equation}
  2. Parsevalâ€™s identity.
    Let \(B=(v_1,v_2,\dots, v_n)\) be an orthonormal basis of \(V\text{.}\) Given any \(v=\sum_{i=1}^nc_iv_i\in V\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{align} \norm{v}^2\amp = \abs{c_1}^2+\abs{c_2}^2+\cdots +\abs{c_n}^2\tag{1.70}\\ \amp = \abs{\angvec{v,v_1}}^2+\abs{\angvec{v,v_2}}^2+\cdots +\abs{\angvec{v,v_n}}^2\text{.}\tag{1.71} \end{align}
